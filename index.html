Frequently Asked

Questions buyers ask before they engage.

Answered directly. If your question isn't here, send it to us and we'll respond the same day.

What is the minimum shipment value you work with?

We typically work with importers on shipments valued at USD 25,000 and above. For first engagements, no commercial commitment is required until you have reviewed and approved the supplier shortlist and qualification report. Contact us with your requirement and we'll confirm whether it falls within our operational scope.

How does the no-retainer model actually work?

The first milestone — supplier shortlist and qualification report — is completed and delivered before any payment is required. You review the shortlist, ask questions, and only proceed (and pay) when you're satisfied with the quality of the work. Subsequent milestones — PO issuance, freight booking, customs clearance — are billed against execution, not time.

What happens if a supplier fails quality inspection?

Pre-shipment inspection is managed before goods are released for freight booking. If inspection results are below specification, we do not release the cargo — we escalate to the supplier, document the discrepancy, and coordinate remediation. You are informed immediately with the inspection report and a proposed resolution path.

Do you work with Gulf and Saudi importers specifically?

Yes. We have specific experience with UAE free zone operations, Jebel Ali import procedures, Saudi SASO certification requirements, and Arabic-language supplier coordination. Many of our active engagements run on South Asia–Gulf corridors. We can be reached directly via WhatsApp for Gulf clients.

What freight carriers and customs brokers do you use?

We coordinate through established freight forwarders across primary corridors, selecting carriers and brokers based on route, timeline, cargo type, and your delivery requirement. We do not restrict to a single carrier — this allows us to book to your window rather than available inventory. Specific partners for your corridor are disclosed at the proposal stage.

Can you handle Saudi SASO certification and import requirements?

Yes. SASO product conformity certificates, IECEE CB documentation, and Saudi standards requirements are managed as part of our Gulf trade coordination. We prepare and verify all required documentation at origin before shipment, coordinating with accredited certification bodies where required.

What documentation do you provide at close-out?

On close, you receive a complete archived set: supplier qualification report, PO and production milestone records, pre-shipment inspection report, bill of lading, packing list, commercial invoice, customs declaration, and certificate of origin where applicable. All documents are held in a structured archive available on request.

How quickly can you respond to an urgent sourcing requirement?

For urgent requirements, call or WhatsApp us directly — we respond within the business hour for active corridor requests. Standard enquiries receive a response within one business day with an initial scope assessment. Time-critical freight (air, express courier) can be booked within 24 hours of PO confirmation.
